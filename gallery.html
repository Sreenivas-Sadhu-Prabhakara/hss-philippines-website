Our community in pictures

Gallery

A window into the warmth of our gatherings. As our community here takes shape, this album will grow event by event — always shared with care, and with the consent of the families pictured.

Our gallery is where the spirit of the shakha (the weekly gathering) comes to life — children laughing through a game of kho-kho, families raising their arms in Surya Namaskar, the glow of lamps at an utsav (festival).

As our community in the Philippines is only just beginning, we are building this album honestly, one gathering at a time. Rather than fill this page with stock imagery or borrowed pictures, we would rather it grow slowly and truthfully — so that everything you see here really is us. Photographs from our local gatherings will be added event by event, always shared with care and with the consent of the families pictured.

Until then, the tiles below stand ready — a set of albums waiting to be filled as our story unfolds.

With care

Photo consent & family privacy

A gathering is a place of trust, and we treat every photograph the same way. Before a picture of anyone appears here, we ask for their consent — and for children, we ask their parents or guardians first. If you would ever prefer that a photograph be taken down, a quiet word to our volunteers is all it takes.

We share images to convey the warmth of our community, never to single anyone out. Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam — the world is one family — begins with looking after one another's dignity and privacy.

  • Consent firstWe photograph and publish only with the agreement of those pictured.
  • Extra care for childrenPhotos of children appear only with a parent's or guardian's permission.
  • Remove on requestAsk us at any time and we will take a photograph down, no questions asked.
  • Shared to upliftImages are used to celebrate our community, never to embarrass or expose anyone.
