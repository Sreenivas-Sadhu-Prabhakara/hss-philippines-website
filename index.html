Welcome

A warm, wholesome gathering for the whole family

Hindu Swayamsevak Sangh (HSS) is a voluntary, non-profit, cultural and educational community of Hindus living outside India.

At the heart of everything we do is the shakha (branch) — a weekly gathering where children, youth and elders come together for yoga, games, songs, stories and selfless service. It is a place to build good character, stay rooted in our heritage, and contribute, shoulder to shoulder with our neighbours, to the wider Filipino community.

HSS is strictly non-political, run entirely by volunteers, and free of any membership fee. Everyone who shares our values is welcome.

Our three core values

Three simple ideas guide everything we do

Practised together, week after week, through the shakha.

Sanskar

Values & character

The nurturing of good character — cultivating virtues like truth, compassion, self-discipline and selflessness. It is the cornerstone of a well-lived life, and the starting point of all we do.

Seva

Selfless service

Service to all humanity, offered with compassion and without any expectation of reward — from health and yoga days to helping neighbours in need. Nar Seva, Narayan Seva.

Sanghatan

Unity in diversity

The strength of a diverse community united by shared purpose — not one that looks the same, but one that stands together with respect. Our well-being is inseparable from one another's.

The shakha

An hour and a half, once a week, for every generation

A shakha follows a gentle three-part rhythm. We begin with the physical (sharirik) — yoga, Surya Namaskar and joyful team games like kabaddi and kho-kho. We move to the intellectual (bauddhik) — discussions, stories of inspiring figures, and songs (geet). And we close with prayer (prarthana), offered for the well-being of all and for peace in the world.

There are no fees, everyone is welcome, and children take part alongside their parents — because the best way to learn good values is to see them lived.

HSS in the Philippines

A community taking shape — and it has begun

HSS in the Philippines is new and forming — a small circle of families and volunteers gathering around these values, and warmly inviting others to join.

We now hold two regular shakhas in Metro Manila: our main family gathering on Sunday mornings at the Hindu temple in Paco, and the Chhatrapati Shivaji Shakha for children and young people on Saturday evenings at the Crimarcel basketball court. Everyone is welcome, and there is no fee.

Earlier in 2026, volunteers here also joined the wider Hindu community in supporting the Chant Mahaveer celebration in Manila and in observing the International Day of Yoga in Makati. There is a place in this story for you.
